Who I am

About Me

I am a first-year PhD student in Psychological Health and Learning Sciences (Measurement, Quantitative Methods and Learning Sciences) at the University of Houston, where I am affiliated with the I AM Lab (Dr. Allison Master) and the "Becoming" Lab (Dr. Virginia Snodgrass Rangel).

My research sits at the intersection of educational psychology, science identity, and equity. I investigate how stereotypes, gender, belonging, and impostor feelings shape students' engagement in STEM — and how we can design learning environments that counteract those barriers.

Before Houston, I taught Biology at Aakash Educational Services in India for three years, and completed an EdM in Curriculum & Instruction in MSE Education at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. My academic roots are in biochemistry (Hyderabad Central University, Calcutta University).

Outside research, I paint, sketch, and write — a few of my artworks have been published in magazines.
